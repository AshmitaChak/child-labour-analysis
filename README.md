## ğŸ“Š Key Insights

Child labour prevalence has declined across most regions over time, though the pace and magnitude of progress vary significantly by region.

A small number of countries account for a disproportionate share of children engaged in labour, with patterns differing by age group.

Gender disparities persist across many countries, with boys generally exhibiting higher child labour participation rates than girls.
